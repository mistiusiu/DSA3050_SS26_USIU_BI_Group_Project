Social media platforms such as Twitter have become essential tools for organizations to communicate with their audiences, promote services, and build brand visibility. Destination Marketing Organizations (DMOs), in particular, rely heavily on Twitter to engage potential tourists, share promotional content, and maintain public interest in travel destinations. Engagement on these platforms measured through likes, replies, and retweets serves as a key indicator of how effectively content resonates with users.

Despite the availability of large volumes of social media data, it remains unclear which specific factors drive higher engagement. Tweet performance can be influenced by multiple variables, including content type (e.g., image, video, or text), posting time, emotional tone, linguistic features, and the size or influence of the account posting the content. Additionally, external conditions such as different time periods (e.g., pre-COVID, lockdown, and post-lockdown phases) may also impact how audiences interact with content. Understanding these factors is crucial for organizations seeking to optimize their communication strategies and maximize audience reach.

This project addresses the challenge of identifying the key drivers of social media engagement by analyzing a dataset of tweets collected from Destination Marketing Organizations. The dataset contains information on engagement metrics, sentiment scores, linguistic characteristics, posting time, and audience attributes such as follower count. However, the raw dataset contains inconsistencies such as redundant columns, unclear naming conventions, and mixed data types, which must be addressed before meaningful analysis can be performed.

The objective of this project is therefore twofold: first, to clean, transform, and model the dataset into a structured format suitable for analysis; and second, to explore and identify patterns that explain how different factors influence tweet engagement. By applying data modeling techniques and analytical methods, the project aims to uncover insights into how content strategy, timing, sentiment, and audience characteristics affect user interaction on social media.

The findings from this analysis can support organizations in making data-driven decisions about their social media strategies. Specifically, it can help DMOs determine the most effective types of content to post, the optimal times for posting, and how to tailor messaging to maximize engagement. Ultimately, the project contributes to a better understanding of how social media communication strategies can be optimized to improve audience engagement and overall digital marketing effectiveness.
